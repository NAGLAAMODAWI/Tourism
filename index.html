Jebel Barkal

Jebel Barkal is one of the most famous archaeological sites in Sudan, where it has been closely linked to religious traditions since prehistoric times, and then formed the religious capital of the historical kingdom of Napata. UNESCO World Heritage in 2003.

Sanganeb Island

Sanganeb is one of the Sudanese islands located in the Red Sea. This island is located at a distance of thirteen nautical miles from the port of Port Sudan in the eastern coasts of the Sudanese Red Sea State. It is considered one of the most beautiful marine islands, as it extends over six kilometers and two kilometers in width. The island is a natural marine reserve and is the first marine reserve in Sudan. It has been included in a World Heritage Site

Meroe

Meroe, or “Bajrawi” is the old name for the city that contains the Nubian pyramids in Shendi Governorate in the Sudanese River Nile State, about 200 km from Khartoum. It was the capital of the Kushite kingdom and contains the pyramids of Nubia. The pyramids built by the Kushite rulers in (Sudan) in 300 BC.
